Key Insight: Tools Are Table Stakes

Salesforce appears in 14.8% of job descriptions—but that's not a differentiator anymore. It's assumed. What's more telling is the rise of revenue intelligence tools like Gong and Clari. Companies want leaders who can operationalize data, not just manage pipelines.

MEDDIC/MEDDPICC now shows up in 31 postings explicitly. Two years ago, methodology requirements were rare in job postings. Now they're screening criteria for enterprise roles.

Key Insight: AI Is Now Expected

28.0% of postings mention AI or machine learning—and these aren't just AI companies. Traditional enterprise sales roles now expect leaders who can leverage AI in their sales motion: forecasting, lead scoring, conversation intelligence, pipeline analysis.

If you can't articulate how you've used AI tools to improve sales outcomes, you're already behind.

What These Red Flags Actually Mean

"Competitive compensation" (164 postings): Without disclosed ranges, this typically means below-market or highly variable comp. Companies confident in their offers publish them.

"Fast-paced environment": Often signals understaffing or constant fire-drills. Ask about team size and recent turnover.

"Self-starter" / "Wear many hats": Common at Seed/Series A, but a red flag at Series B+. Usually means you'll lack RevOps support and spend time on admin work.

50%+ travel: At VP level, this suggests territory is geographically spread without regional structure, or the company hasn't invested in inside sales.
