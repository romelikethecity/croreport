Company Overview

What Is Sybill?

Sybill is an AI sales assistant that focuses on what they call "mid-deal execution"‚Äîthe work that happens between calls. Founded in 2020, the company has raised $14M and is backed by investors including Neotribe Ventures.

Unlike Gong, which is primarily a manager tool for call coaching and forecasting, Sybill is built for individual AEs. It captures data from calls, emails, Slack, and CRM, then uses AI to automate the busywork: updating CRM fields, writing follow-up emails, preparing for meetings, and tracking deal progress.

The key differentiator is their "single-agent architecture"‚Äîinstead of having disconnected AI tools for different tasks, Sybill maintains shared context across all workflows. This means your follow-up email actually references what was discussed in the call, and your CRM notes reflect the real conversation.

Limitations

Where Sybill Falls Short

Coaching Features Are Basic

If your primary need is manager call coaching‚Äîscorecards, talk ratios, comparative rep performance‚ÄîGong is significantly better. Sybill's coaching features exist but aren't the focus.

Newer Platform

Sybill is younger than Gong with a smaller customer base. That means fewer integrations, less documentation, and potentially more rough edges. They're iterating fast, but enterprise teams may want more maturity.

Pricing Not Transparent

Like most sales tools, Sybill doesn't publish pricing. The 14-day free trial is genuinely useful for evaluation, but you'll need to talk to sales for actual costs.

The Bottom Line

Sybill is carving out a smart niche: the AI sales assistant that helps reps, not managers. If your CRM is a graveyard of stale data and your AEs spend hours on follow-up emails, Sybill directly addresses those pain points at a fraction of Gong's cost.

It's not a Gong killer‚Äîit's a different tool for a different problem. The best teams might use both. Start with the 14-day free trial to see if the CRM automation and AI follow-ups actually save your reps time.
