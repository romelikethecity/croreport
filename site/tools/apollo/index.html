Company Overview

What Is Apollo.io?

Apollo.io is an all-in-one sales intelligence and engagement platform. It combines a B2B contact database with email sequencing, dialer functionality, and CRM integrations. Founded in 2015 by Tim Zheng and Ray Li, the company has raised over $250M and serves over 1 million users globally.

The core value proposition is simple: instead of paying $15K+ for ZoomInfo (data) plus $10K+ for Outreach (sequences), you get both in one platform starting at $49/user/month. For startups and growing teams, this bundled approach removes friction and keeps costs manageable.

Apollo built its database through a combination of web scraping, public data aggregation, and a contributor network (users who connect their email accounts contribute anonymized data). This approach lets them offer competitive pricing but means data quality can vary, especially for smaller companies or niche industries.

The Bundled Approach

Apollo's strategy is deliberate: combine data + engagement in one tool and price aggressively to win market share. They've grown to over 1M users by being the obvious choice for teams who can't afford separate ZoomInfo + Outreach licenses. The trade-off is data depth and enterprise-grade features.

The Free Tier Is Legitimately Useful

Unlike most "free trials," Apollo's free tier lets you actually do prospecting work. 60 mobile credits and 120 exports per year is enough for a founder or solo SDR to validate whether the platform works for them. No credit card required.

Credit System Explained

Apollo uses credits for different actions:

  • Email credits: Unlimited on all paid plans (rate limits apply)
  • Mobile credits: Used when revealing direct phone numbers
  • Export credits: Used when exporting contacts to CSV or CRM

The credit system is more generous than ZoomInfo's, but you'll still need to plan usage. Most teams find the Professional tier ($99/user/month) hits the sweet spot between credits and features.

Limitations

Where Apollo.io Falls Short

Apollo offers great value, but you're making real trade-offs compared to enterprise tools. Here's what you need to know:

Data Quality Isn't ZoomInfo-Level

Apollo's database is large but not as accurate or deep as ZoomInfo's. Email bounce rates tend to be higher, and direct dial coverage is weaker, especially for senior executives and niche industries.

"The data is hit or miss. For common roles at tech companies, it's pretty good. But when I'm prospecting into manufacturing or healthcare, I'm seeing 30-40% bounce rates. You get what you pay for."
‚Äî SDR, Mid-Market SaaS, G2 Reviews

Enterprise Features Are Limited

If you need advanced security controls, custom SLAs, dedicated support, or complex permission structures, Apollo falls short. The Organization tier adds some enterprise features, but it's not designed for Fortune 500 procurement processes.

"We loved Apollo at 15 people but outgrew it at 50. The territory management is basic, the API limits became a problem, and our security team needed features Apollo doesn't have. We had to move to ZoomInfo."
‚Äî RevOps Lead, Reddit

Sequences Aren't Outreach/Salesloft Quality

Apollo's built-in sequences are good enough for most teams, but power users will notice the gaps: fewer customization options, less sophisticated A/B testing, and reporting that doesn't match dedicated engagement platforms.

üîç Questions to Ask Before Signing

  1. What's the email bounce rate for my target market? Ask for data accuracy metrics specific to your ICP. Test with the free tier first.
  2. How many credits do I actually need? Calculate your prospecting volume and map to credit tiers. Upgrading mid-contract is possible but not ideal.
  3. Will the sequence functionality replace our current tool? If you're on Outreach or Salesloft, test Apollo's sequences before committing to consolidation.
  4. What's the direct dial coverage for my personas? If phone is a major channel, verify Apollo has numbers for your target titles and industries.
  5. What integrations do we need? Check that Apollo connects to your CRM, dialer, and other tools natively or via Zapier.
  6. What happens when we scale? Understand credit overages, API limits, and whether the Organization tier meets your growth needs.
