Context: Chorus is Part of ZoomInfo

ZoomInfo acquired Chorus.ai in July 2021 for $575 million. Chorus now operates as part of ZoomInfo's "GTM Platform," which includes data (ZoomInfo), engagement (Engage), and conversation intelligence (Chorus). Pricing and packaging are increasingly bundled, making Chorus most attractive for existing ZoomInfo customers.

Company Overview

From conversation intelligence pioneer to ZoomInfo's analytics play.

The Chorus Story

Chorus.ai was founded in 2015 in San Francisco by Roy Raanani and Micha Breakstone, competing directly with Gong in the emerging conversation intelligence category. The company raised $85 million before being acquired by ZoomInfo in July 2021 for $575 million.

Pre-acquisition, Chorus was a strong Gong competitor with solid call recording, transcription, and coaching capabilities. Post-acquisition, the product has been integrated into ZoomInfo's broader go-to-market platform, with development focused on data synergies rather than standalone CI innovation.

Today, Chorus remains a capable tool but has ceded market leadership to Gong, which continues to invest heavily in AI and expand its feature set. Chorus's value proposition centers on integration with ZoomInfo's data and engagement tools rather than best-in-class conversation intelligence.

Pricing Deep Dive

Chorus pricing varies significantly based on ZoomInfo relationship.

What You'll Actually Pay

Chorus pricing has two distinct tracks: bundled with ZoomInfo (most common) or standalone. ZoomInfo customers typically get better Chorus pricing as part of platform deals.

Scenario Typical Range Notes
ZoomInfo Bundle $50-80/user/mo Best pricing; requires ZoomInfo commitment
Standalone $100-130/user/mo Comparable to Gong; harder to justify
ZoomInfo Advanced (Bundle) Included Some ZoomInfo tiers include Chorus
Implementation $0-3,000 Simpler than Gong; often waived in bundles
Minimum Contract Annual (typically) Often tied to ZoomInfo contract length

Chorus vs. Gong Pricing

Standalone, Chorus and Gong are comparably priced (~$100-160/user/month). The economic argument for Chorus only works in two scenarios: (1) you're bundling with ZoomInfo, or (2) you need fewer features and can negotiate a lower rate.

"We evaluated Chorus standalone and the price was basically the same as Gong. At that point, we went with Gong because the AI features were clearly ahead. Chorus only makes sense at a discount."

Core Features

Solid conversation intelligence with ZoomInfo integration advantages.

Call Recording 8/10

Reliable call recording across Zoom, Teams, Google Meet, and more. Automatic capture with good audio quality and speaker separation.

Transcription 8/10

Accurate transcription with decent speaker identification. Supports search across call libraries and keyword tracking.

ZoomInfo Integration 9/10

Native integration with ZoomInfo data enriches call context with prospect information. Unique advantage over Gong if you're in the ecosystem.

Deal Intelligence 7/10

Deal tracking and risk scoring based on call activity. Functional but not as sophisticated as Gong's AI-powered insights.

Coaching Tools 7/10

Comments, snippets, and playlists for coaching. Standard features but UI feels dated compared to Gong's polish.

AI Capabilities 6/10

AI-generated summaries and insights exist but lag Gong significantly. Post-acquisition investment has focused elsewhere.

Where Chorus Falls Short

The honest gaps every buyer should understand.

The Gong Gap

Pre-acquisition, Chorus was a genuine Gong competitor. Post-acquisition, the product has fallen behind on innovation while Gong invested heavily in AI, forecasting, and market expansion.

AI & Innovation 6/10

Gong has pulled ahead significantly on AI capabilities. Chorus's summaries and insights feel a generation behind.

User Experience 7/10

Functional but not delightful. Interface feels less polished than Gong, and navigation can be clunky.

Standalone Value 6/10

Hard to justify at standalone pricing when Gong offers more for similar cost. Value depends on ZoomInfo bundle.

Market Momentum 6/10

ZoomInfo's focus has shifted to broader GTM platform. Chorus development appears to have slowed.

Decision Framework

Clear guidance on when Chorus makes sense.

Buy Chorus If...

  • You're already a ZoomInfo customer
  • Bundle pricing makes it significantly cheaper than Gong
  • ZoomInfo data integration is valuable to you
  • Basic call recording/coaching meets your needs
  • You don't need cutting-edge AI capabilities
  • Budget is a primary constraint

Skip Chorus If...

  • You're not using ZoomInfo (or don't plan to)
  • Best-in-class AI and insights matter
  • You're evaluating standalone at similar pricing to Gong
  • Call coaching is a critical use case
  • You want a platform with strong innovation momentum
  • Market leadership matters for your evaluation

Questions to Ask ZoomInfo/Chorus

Due diligence questions for your evaluation.

  • "What's the pricing with and without ZoomInfo bundled?"
    Understand the true value proposition. Standalone may not make sense.
  • "What's on the Chorus product roadmap for 2026?"
    Assess whether ZoomInfo is investing in Chorus innovation or just maintenance.
  • "How does your AI compare to Gong's current capabilities?"
    Get honest answers about feature parity. AI is where Gong leads significantly.
  • "What integrations beyond ZoomInfo do you support?"
    If you're not using ZoomInfo, integration value diminishes.
  • "Can we get a pilot to compare with Gong side-by-side?"
    Real-world comparison is the best way to evaluate. Most vendors accommodate pilots.

The Bottom Line

Chorus is a solid conversation intelligence tool that makes excellent sense for ZoomInfo customers getting it bundled. The ZoomInfo integration is genuinely valuable, and the bundle pricing can be 40-50% cheaper than standalone Gong.

But as a standalone product competing head-to-head with Gong at similar pricing, Chorus no longer wins on merit. Gong's AI capabilities, user experience, and innovation pace have pulled ahead significantly since the 2021 acquisition.

The decision framework is simple: ZoomInfo customer? Consider Chorus. Everyone else? Start with Gong.
