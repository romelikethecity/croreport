## ü§ñ The AI Narrative is Now Non-Negotiable

The most striking data point this week: **AI/Machine Learning mentions (403) now exceed SaaS (261) by 54%.**

This isn't a gradual shift. Six months ago, SaaS dominated job descriptions. Now AI appears in nearly a third of all sales leadership postings. The signal is clear: even if you're not selling AI products directly, employers want leaders who understand how AI transforms the sales function.

What this looks like in practice:

- "Experience implementing AI-powered sales tools" appearing in VP job descriptions
- "Understanding of AI/ML applications in [industry]" as a nice-to-have becoming a must-have
- GTM roles at non-AI companies explicitly asking for "AI GTM experience"

If your resume and interview talking points haven't evolved to include AI fluency, you're already behind. This doesn't mean fabricating expertise. It means having a perspective on how AI changes prospecting, qualification, forecasting, and coaching.

## üè• Healthcare is Hiring Like It's 2021

**Healthcare and Technology are now tied at 704 job mentions each.** That's not a typo.

For years, tech sales leaders have operated as if their only career path is company-to-company within SaaS. The data says otherwise. Healthcare (704), Education (613), Financial Services (566), and even Government (415) are absorbing sales leadership talent at rates that match or exceed pure technology companies.

Why this matters:

- **Larger addressable market for your career.** If you're only looking at "tech" companies, you're ignoring half the opportunity.
- **Different skills transfer more than you think.** Enterprise sales motion, stakeholder management, long cycles? Healthcare has all of that. The product changes. The job doesn't.
- **Less competition.** Every laid-off SaaS VP is applying to the same 50 late-stage startups. Meanwhile, healthcare systems and EdTech companies are struggling to find commercial leaders who understand modern GTM.

The smart move: stop filtering your job search by "industry: technology" and start filtering by "company type: growth-oriented."
